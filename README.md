# hey

I'm Aaron. I'm a lawyer who keeps building software instead of taking weekends off.

I work on legal AI, contracts, and the problem of getting a computer to be useful without letting it confidently make everything worse. I use LLMs a lot. I do not believe them about anything.

This is a map, not a complete list. A lot of the projects below are experiments; some are more polished than others. That’s life.

## legal AI / things that need to be checked

- [redline-gym](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/redline-gym) — contract-review training environments with known mistakes in them
- [prime-legal-envs](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/prime-legal-envs) — corporate-law and governance environments for testing models
- [m8-market-structure-rails](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/m8-market-structure-rails) — work around crypto market-structure regulation
- [gdpr-dpa-art28-env](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/gdpr-dpa-art28-env) — a DPA review environment built around Article 28

## software for legal work

- [LeBlox](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/LeBlox) — an attempt to keep client work where it belongs
- [counselbridge](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/counselbridge) — legal chat with citations and a human escape hatch
- [plainclause](https://github.com/narcolepticchicken/plainclause) — explain a clause without shipping the whole contract off to who-knows-where
